# How Much Tax Will I Pay in the UK 2025?

**"How much tax will I pay?"** is one of the most common questions UK taxpayers ask. This guide provides exact calculations for the 2025-2026 tax year, with real examples for different salary levels.

## Quick Answer: UK Tax Rates 2025-2026

- **Income Tax**: 0% on first £12,570, then 20%, 40%, or 45%
- **National Insurance**: 12% on earnings £12,570-£50,270, then 2%
- **Combined Rate**: Effectively 32% for most earners (20% + 12%)

## How Much Tax on Different UK Salaries in 2025?

### £20,000 Salary - How Much Tax?

**Annual Breakdown:**
- Gross Salary: £20,000
- Personal Allowance: -£12,570  
- Taxable Income: £7,430
- **Income Tax**: £7,430 × 20% = £1,486
- **National Insurance**: £7,430 × 12% = £892
- **Total Tax**: £2,378
- **Take-Home Pay**: £17,622 (£1,469 per month)

- **National Insurance**: £17,430 × 12% = £2,092
- **Total Tax**: £5,578

## What Affects How Much Tax You Pay?

### 1. Your Tax Code
- **1257L**: Standard tax code for 2025-2026
- **K Codes**: If you owe tax from previous years
- **0T**: No personal allowance (temporary codes)

### 2. Pension Contributions
- **Workplace Pension**: Usually 5% employee, 3% employer minimum
- **Tax Relief**: Reduces your taxable income
- **Example**: £40k salary with £2k pension = tax on £38k only

## Scottish Tax Rates: How Much Tax in Scotland 2025?

Scottish taxpayers pay different income tax rates:

**Scottish Income Tax Rates 2025-2026:**
- **Personal Allowance**: £12,570 (same as rest of UK)
- **Starter Rate**: 19% on £12,571 - £14,876
- **Basic Rate**: 20% on £14,877 - £26,561  
- **Intermediate Rate**: 21% on £26,562 - £43,662
- **Higher Rate**: 42% on £43,663 - £125,140
- **Top Rate**: 47% on earnings over £125,140

**Example: £30,000 in Scotland**
- Starter Rate: £2,305 × 19% = £438
- Basic Rate: £11,638 × 20% = £2,328
- Intermediate Rate: £3,487 × 21% = £732
- **Total Income Tax**: £3,498 (vs £3,486 in England)

## How Much Tax Do Self-Employed Pay in 2025?

Self-employed individuals pay:

### Class 2 National Insurance
- **Rate**: £3.45 per week
- **Threshold**: Only if profits over £6,515

### Class 4 National Insurance  
- **Rate**: 9% on profits £12,570 - £50,270, then 2%
- **No Employment Allowance** (unlike employees)

### Income Tax
- **Same rates** as employed individuals
- **Paid via self-assessment** (not PAYE)

**Example: £40,000 Self-Employed**
- Income Tax: £5,486
- Class 2 NI: £179.40 (52 weeks × £3.45)
- Class 4 NI: (£40,000 - £12,570) × 9% = £2,469
- **Total Tax**: £8,134 (vs £8,778 for employees)

## Quick Tax Calculation Formula

```
Take-Home Pay = Gross Salary 
- Income Tax 
- National Insurance 
- Student Loan (if applicable)
- Pension Contributions (if applicable)
```

### Simple Calculator Method:
1. **Subtract personal allowance** (£12,570) from gross salary
2. **Multiply by 20%** for basic rate taxpayers
3. **Add National Insurance**: Multiply by 12% (most earners)
4. **Subtract from gross salary**
