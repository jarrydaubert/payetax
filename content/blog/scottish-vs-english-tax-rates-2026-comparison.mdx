> **The crossover point: £28,867.** Above this, Scottish taxpayers pay more. Which means if you're earning above this threshold and considering a move, tax should be part of your calculation.

## Why Scotland's Different

Scotland sets its own income tax rates. Six bands instead of England's three.

The idea? More progressive taxation. Lower earners pay less, higher earners pay more.

Whether that's "fair" depends on your politics. Whether it costs you money depends on your salary. Let's look at the actual numbers.

## The Tax Bands: Side by Side

### Scotland (6 Bands)

| Band | Income | Rate |
|------|--------|------|
| Personal Allowance | £0 - £12,570 | 0% |
| Starter | £12,571 - £14,876 | 19% |
| Basic | £14,877 - £26,561 | 20% |
| Intermediate | £26,562 - £43,662 | 21% |
| Higher | £43,663 - £75,000 | 42% |
| Advanced | £75,001 - £125,140 | 45% |
| Top | Over £125,140 | 48% |

### England, Wales & Northern Ireland (3 Bands)

| Band | Income | Rate |
|------|--------|------|
| Personal Allowance | £0 - £12,570 | 0% |
| Basic | £12,571 - £50,270 | 20% |
| Higher | £50,271 - £125,140 | 40% |
| Additional | Over £125,140 | 45% |

> Notice the gap: Scotland hits 42% at £43,663. England stays at 20% until £50,270. That's where the pain starts—and it's a £6,607 income band where Scottish taxpayers pay more than double the rate.

## The Hidden Offset: Council Tax

Here's what the headlines miss.

> Scottish council tax is typically £648/year cheaper than England.

**Average Band D:**
- Scotland: £1,417
- England: £2,065

So if you're earning £50k and paying £179 more in income tax, you're still £469 ahead after council tax. Which means the *real* crossover point is higher than income tax alone suggests.

For someone earning £60k, the £1,500 extra income tax roughly equals the council tax savings—making the total tax burden similar. Above £60k, England pulls ahead even accounting for council tax.

## Who Pays Scottish Tax?

Your workplace doesn't matter. Your home does.

**You pay Scottish tax if:**
- Your main residence is in Scotland
- You spend more time in Scotland than elsewhere in the UK
- Scotland is where your closest ties are

> Live in Edinburgh, work remotely for a London company? Scottish tax.
> Live in Newcastle, commute to Edinburgh? English tax.

Your tax code tells the story: Scottish codes start with "S" (like S1257L).

## What About National Insurance?

Same across the whole UK. No Scottish NI, no English NI.

- **Employee:** 8% on £12,570-£50,270, then 2%
- **Employer:** 13.8% above £9,100

The difference is purely income tax. Which means the comparison we've shown above captures the full employee-side difference.

## Smart Moves for Scottish Taxpayers

### 1. Pension Contributions Hit Harder

Scotland's higher rates mean bigger relief:

| Your Rate | England Relief | Scotland Relief |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Basic | 20% | 20% |
| Intermediate | - | 21% |
| Higher | 40% | 42% |
| Advanced | - | 45% |
| Top/Additional | 45% | 48% |

> Earning £60k in Scotland? Every £1,000 into your pension saves £420. In England? £400. Which means Scottish higher-rate taxpayers get 5% better pension tax relief.

Use salary sacrifice. The maths works even better—you save NI too.

### 2. Watch Your Bonus Timing

A £10k bonus pushing you from £40k to £50k?

- **Scotland:** £4,200 tax on that bonus (42%)
- **England:** £2,000 tax on that bonus (20%)

> That's £2,200 difference on one bonus. Which means timing when bonuses hit your payslip matters more for Scottish taxpayers.

If you can defer a bonus to a year when you're in a lower band, it could save thousands.

### 3. Marriage Allowance Still Works

Transfer £1,260 of personal allowance. Save £252/year.

Works identically in Scotland and England. [Claim it here](https://www.gov.uk/marriage-allowance) or [use our calculator](/tools/marriage-allowance-calculator).

## Should You Move for Tax?

Let's be honest.

### The Maths Says...

Earning £75k+ and considering a move south?

- **Tax savings:** £5,000 - £8,000/year
- **Over 30 years:** £150,000 - £240,000

That's real money. Enough to retire earlier or leave a bigger inheritance.

### But Also Consider...

- **House prices:** Often £100k-£300k higher outside Scotland
- **Cost of living:** London/South East significantly more expensive
- **Quality of life:** Commute times, space, lifestyle
- **Family:** Can't put a price on being near people you love

> Saving £7k/year on tax means nothing if you're spending £15k more on a mortgage. Which means a move purely for tax rarely makes sense—but if you're relocating anyway, factor it in.

For most people under £50k? Don't move for tax. The difference is negligible.

For £100k+? Worth a serious conversation with an accountant.
