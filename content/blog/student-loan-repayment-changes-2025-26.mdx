# Student Loan Repayment Changes 2025-26: Everything You Need to Know

**Are you a UK graduate wondering how student loan changes affect your repayments in 2025-26?** This comprehensive guide covers all the key updates to thresholds, rates, and calculation methods across all student loan plans, with real salary examples.

## Quick Summary: What's Changed in 2025-26

- **Plan 2 Loans:** Threshold remains at £27,295 (most graduates)
- **Plan 5 Loans:** 30-year forgiveness period (reduced from 40 years)
- **Interest Rates:** Capped during high inflation periods
- **Plan 1 & 4:** Minor threshold adjustments
- **Postgraduate:** Unchanged threshold and rates

## Understanding Your Student Loan Plan

### Which Plan Am I On?

Your student loan plan depends on **when and where** you studied:

- **Plan 1:** Started before September 2012 (England/Wales) or before 1998 (Scotland/Northern Ireland)
- **Plan 2:** Started September 2012+ in England/Wales
- **Plan 4:** Started 1998+ in Scotland  
- **Plan 5:** Started September 2023+ in England/Wales
- **Postgraduate:** Master's/PhD loans from 2016+

*Check your loan details on the [Student Loans Company website](https://www.gov.uk/sign-in-to-manage-your-student-loan-balance) if unsure.*

## Plan 2 Student Loans (Most Common)

**Plan 2 covers:** Most English and Welsh students who started university from September 2012 onwards.

### 2025-26 Plan 2 Details

- **Repayment Threshold:** £27,295 annually (£2,274 monthly, £525 weekly)
- **Repayment Rate:** 9% on income above threshold
- **Interest Rate:** RPI + 0-3% (currently capped at 7.3%)
- **Forgiveness Period:** 30 years from first repayment

### Plan 2 Salary Examples

#### £25,000 Salary
- **Income above threshold:** £0 (below £27,295)
- **Monthly repayment:** £0
- **Annual repayment:** £0

#### £30,000 Salary
- **Income above threshold:** £2,705
- **Monthly repayment:** £20.29 (9% of £2,705 ÷ 12)
- **Annual repayment:** £243

#### £35,000 Salary  
- **Income above threshold:** £7,705
- **Monthly repayment:** £57.79
- **Annual repayment:** £693

#### £50,000 Salary
- **Income above threshold:** £22,705
- **Monthly repayment:** £170.29
- **Annual repayment:** £2,043

#### £75,000 Salary
- **Income above threshold:** £47,705
- **Monthly repayment:** £357.79
- **Annual repayment:** £4,293

## Plan 1 Student Loans (Pre-2012)

**Plan 1 covers:** Students who started before September 2012 in England/Wales, or anytime in Scotland/Northern Ireland before Plan 4 introduction.

### 2025-26 Plan 1 Details

- **Repayment Threshold:** £22,015 annually (£1,834 monthly, £423 weekly)
- **Repayment Rate:** 9% on income above threshold
- **Interest Rate:** Lower of RPI or Bank of England base rate + 1%
- **Forgiveness Period:** 25 years from first repayment OR age 65

### Plan 1 vs Plan 2 Comparison

**£40,000 Salary Example:**

**Plan 1 Repayment:**
- Income above £22,015: £17,985
- **Annual repayment:** £1,619 (9% × £17,985)
- **Monthly repayment:** £135

**Plan 2 Repayment:**
- Income above £27,295: £12,705  
- **Annual repayment:** £1,143 (9% × £12,705)
- **Monthly repayment:** £95

**Plan 1 pays £476 more per year** due to lower threshold.

## Plan 4 Student Loans (Scotland)

**Plan 4 covers:** Scottish students who started from 1998 onwards (replaces Plan 1 in Scotland).

### 2025-26 Plan 4 Details

- **Repayment Threshold:** £27,660 annually (£2,305 monthly, £532 weekly)
- **Repayment Rate:** 9% on income above threshold  
- **Interest Rate:** RPI (Retail Price Index)
- **Forgiveness Period:** 30 years from first repayment

### Plan 4 Advantages

- **Higher threshold** than Plan 2 (£27,660 vs £27,295)
- **Lower interest rates** (RPI only, no additional charges)
- **Scottish graduate benefit:** Slightly less total repayment

**£35,000 Salary - Plan 4:**
- Income above £27,660: £7,340
- **Annual repayment:** £661
- **Monthly repayment:** £55

*(Compare to £693 annually for Plan 2)*

## Plan 5 Student Loans (2023+ Starters)

**Plan 5 covers:** English and Welsh students starting from September 2023.

### 2025-26 Plan 5 Details

- **Repayment Threshold:** £25,000 annually (£2,083 monthly, £481 weekly)
- **Repayment Rate:** 9% on income above threshold
- **Interest Rate:** RPI only (no additional charges)
- **Forgiveness Period:** 30 years (reduced from 40 years)

### Key Plan 5 Changes

**Major Improvement:** The forgiveness period reduced from 40 to 30 years significantly benefits graduates, especially those with larger loans.

**Lower Threshold Impact:** Starting repayments at £25,000 vs £27,295 means earlier repayment starts.

**£30,000 Salary - Plan 5:**
- Income above £25,000: £5,000
- **Annual repayment:** £450
- **Monthly repayment:** £37.50

**£60,000 Salary - Plan 5:**
- Income above £25,000: £35,000
- **Annual repayment:** £3,150
- **Monthly repayment:** £262.50

### Plan 5 vs Plan 2 Lifetime Comparison

**Example: £45,000 starting salary, 3% annual increases:**

**Plan 2 (30-year forgiveness at £27,295):**
- Total repayments: ~£47,000
- Loan forgiven: ~£23,000

**Plan 5 (30-year forgiveness at £25,000):**
- Total repayments: ~£52,000
- Loan forgiven: ~£18,000

*Plan 5 graduates typically repay more due to lower threshold.*

## Postgraduate Student Loans

**Covers:** Master's degree loans (from 2016) and Doctoral loans (from 2018).

### 2025-26 Postgraduate Details

- **Repayment Threshold:** £21,000 annually (£1,750 monthly, £404 weekly)
- **Repayment Rate:** 6% on income above threshold
- **Interest Rate:** RPI + 3% (capped during high inflation)
- **Forgiveness Period:** 30 years from first repayment

### Multiple Loan Repayments

If you have both undergraduate and postgraduate loans, you pay:
- **Undergraduate rate** (9%) on income above undergraduate threshold
- **Additional postgraduate rate** (6%) on income above £21,000

**£50,000 Salary with Plan 2 + Postgraduate:**

**Plan 2 Calculation:**
- £50,000 - £27,295 = £22,705
- Plan 2 repayment: £22,705 × 9% = £2,043

**Postgraduate Calculation:**
- £50,000 - £21,000 = £29,000  
- Postgraduate repayment: £29,000 × 6% = £1,740

**Total Annual Repayment:** £3,783 (£315 per month)

## Interest Rate Changes 2025-26

### Current Interest Rate Structure

**Plan 1 & 4:** Lower of RPI or Bank of England base rate + 1%
- Currently around 3-4%
- Most favorable rates

**Plan 2 & 5:** RPI only (simplified from previous structure)
- No longer varies by income level
- Currently capped at 7.3% during high inflation

**Postgraduate:** RPI + 3%
- Highest interest rates
- Currently capped at 7.3%

### Interest Rate Caps

**Government Protection:** Interest rates are capped to prevent excessive charges during high inflation periods. This particularly benefits:
- Plan 2 borrowers (would previously pay RPI + 3%)
- Postgraduate borrowers
- Recent graduates during high inflation

## Salary Sacrifice and Student Loans

### How Salary Sacrifice Affects Repayments

Student loan repayments are calculated on **gross pay before salary sacrifice deductions**, meaning:

**Pension Contributions:** Reduce both tax/NI AND student loan repayments
**Other Benefits:** May not reduce student loan calculations (check specific schemes)

**Example - £40,000 salary with £2,000 pension contribution:**

**Without Pension:**
- Gross pay: £40,000
- Above Plan 2 threshold: £12,705
- Student loan: £1,143 annually

**With £2,000 Pension:**
- Gross pay: £40,000  
- Student loan calculation: Still on £40,000
- **Student loan: £1,143 annually (unchanged)**
- But saves tax/NI on the £2,000

*Important: Student loans calculated on gross pay, not reduced pay.*

## Regional Variations

### Scottish Graduates Working in England

- **Loan Plan:** Remains Plan 4 (Scottish rates and thresholds)
- **Income Tax:** Pay English rates if English tax resident
- **Student Loan:** Calculate on gross salary using Plan 4 rules

### English Graduates Working in Scotland  

- **Loan Plan:** Remains Plan 2 (English rates and thresholds)
- **Income Tax:** Pay Scottish rates if Scottish tax resident
- **Student Loan:** Calculate on gross salary using Plan 2 rules

## Planning and Optimization Strategies

### Early Repayment Considerations

**Generally NOT recommended** for most borrowers because:
- Loans are written off after 30 years
- Interest rates often below inflation  
- Opportunity cost of investing money elsewhere
- Protection against income reduction

**Consider early repayment if:**
- Very high earner likely to repay in full
- Psychological benefit of being debt-free
- Interest rates exceed investment returns

### Career and Salary Planning

**Threshold Awareness:** Understanding how salary increases affect repayments:

- **Just below threshold:** Small increases have no impact
- **Just above threshold:** Sudden 9% marginal rate
- **High earners:** Effective marginal rates can exceed 50% (tax + NI + student loans)

### Professional Advice

**Seek advice for:**
- Multiple loan types
- Complex employment situations
- Self-employment income
- International tax considerations

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When do I stop paying?

- **Plan 1:** After 25 years OR at age 65
- **Plan 2/4/5:** After 30 years from first repayment
- **Postgraduate:** After 30 years from first repayment
- **All plans:** When balance reaches £0

### What if I move abroad?

- **Still owe repayments** based on worldwide income
- **Must notify SLC** of overseas employment
- **Different threshold** may apply (often lower)
- **Enforcement possible** through international agreements

### Can I pay extra?

- **Yes, but generally not recommended** for most borrowers
- **No early repayment penalties**
- **Consider opportunity cost** vs other investments
- **Psychological vs financial** decision
