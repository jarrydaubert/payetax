# UK Tax Changes 2025-2026: What Every Taxpayer Needs to Know

**The 2025-2026 tax year brings significant changes affecting millions of UK taxpayers.** From updated income tax thresholds to National Insurance modifications and pension contribution limits, here's everything you need to know to prepare your finances.

## Quick Summary: Key Changes for 2025-2026

- **Income Tax**: Personal allowance remains frozen at £12,570
- **National Insurance**: New thresholds and rate adjustments
- **Pension**: Annual allowance increased to £60,000
- **Student Loans**: Updated repayment thresholds for all plans
- **Scottish Tax**: Additional rate bands continue divergence

## Income Tax Changes 2025-2026

### Personal Allowance Freeze Continues

The personal allowance remains **frozen at £12,570** for the fifth consecutive year. This freeze continues until April 2028, meaning more people will pay tax as wages increase with inflation.

**Impact:** If you earn exactly £12,570, you still pay no income tax. However, with average wage growth of 4-6%, more taxpayers will cross into higher tax brackets.

### Income Tax Bands and Rates

**England, Wales & Northern Ireland:**
- Personal Allowance: £0 - £12,570 (0%)
- Basic Rate: £12,571 - £50,270 (20%)
- Higher Rate: £50,271 - £125,140 (40%)
- Additional Rate: Over £125,140 (45%)

**Key Changes:**
- Higher rate threshold remains frozen at £50,270
- Additional rate threshold stays at £125,140
- Personal allowance tapering begins at £100,000 (unchanged)

### High Income Child Benefit Charge

The High Income Child Benefit Charge continues with the same thresholds:
- Charge begins when income exceeds £50,000
- Full charge applied when income reaches £60,000
- Rate: 1% of child benefit for every £100 of income over £50,000

## National Insurance Changes 2025-2026

### Employee National Insurance

**Class 1 National Insurance Rates:**
- Primary Threshold: £12,570 annually (£242 weekly)
- Upper Earnings Limit: £50,270 annually (£967 weekly)
- Rate: 8% on earnings between thresholds, 2% above

**What's New:**
- Thresholds aligned with income tax personal allowance
- No rate changes from 2024-2025
- Class 1A and 1B rates remain at 13.8% for employers

### Self-Employed National Insurance

**Class 2 National Insurance:**
- Small Profits Threshold: £12,570
- Weekly rate: £3.45 (if profits exceed threshold)

**Class 4 National Insurance:**
- Lower Profits Limit: £12,570
- Upper Profits Limit: £50,270
- Rate: 6% on profits between limits, 2% above

## Pension Contribution Changes 2025-2026

### Annual Allowance Increase

**Major Change:** The pension annual allowance increases to **£60,000** for the 2025-2026 tax year, up from £40,000.

**Who Benefits:**
- High earners with significant pension capacity
- Those catching up on missed contributions
- Business owners planning retirement strategies

### Tapered Annual Allowance

The tapered annual allowance still applies to high earners:
- Reduction begins when threshold income exceeds £200,000
- Minimum allowance remains at £10,000
- Tapering rate: £1 reduction for every £2 of adjusted income over threshold

### Lifetime Allowance Abolition

**Abolished:** The lifetime allowance is permanently removed for the 2025-2026 tax year and beyond.

**Impact:**
- No limit on total pension savings growth
- Simplified pension planning for high-net-worth individuals
- Enhanced retirement security for long-term savers

## Student Loan Changes 2025-2026

### Plan 2 Loans (Most Common)

**Updated Repayment Threshold:** £27,295 (increased from £27,295)
- **Rate:** 9% of income above threshold
- **Interest Rate:** RPI + 3% (capped during high inflation)

### Other Student Loan Plans

**Plan 1 (Pre-2012 students):**
- Threshold: £22,015
- Rate: 9% above threshold

**Plan 4 (Scotland):**
- Threshold: £27,660
- Rate: 9% above threshold

**Plan 5 (2023+ students):**
- Threshold: £25,000
- Rate: 9% above threshold
- **New:** 30-year write-off period (reduced from 40 years)

**Postgraduate Loans:**
- Threshold: £21,000
- Rate: 6% above threshold

## Scottish Tax Rates 2025-2026

### Income Tax Rates

Scotland continues its differentiated approach:

- **Starter Rate:** 19% on £12,571 - £14,876
- **Basic Rate:** 20% on £14,877 - £26,561  
- **Intermediate Rate:** 21% on £26,562 - £43,662
- **Higher Rate:** 42% on £43,663 - £75,000
- **Advanced Rate:** 45% on £75,001 - £125,140
- **Top Rate:** 48% on income over £125,140

**Key Difference:** Scottish taxpayers pay significantly more tax on higher incomes compared to the rest of the UK.

## Marriage Allowance 2025-2026

### Eligibility and Amount

- **Transfer Amount:** £1,260 (10% of personal allowance)
- **Maximum Saving:** £252 per year
- **Eligibility:** Non-taxpayer can transfer to basic-rate taxpaying spouse/civil partner

### Application Process

- Apply online through HMRC
- Can backdate up to 4 tax years
- Automatic renewal unless circumstances change

## Capital Gains Tax 2025-2026

### Annual Exempt Amount

**Significant Reduction:** The CGT annual exempt amount reduces to **£3,000** for 2025-2026, down from £6,000 in 2024-2025.

### Tax Rates
- **Basic Rate Taxpayers:** 10% (18% for residential property)
- **Higher/Additional Rate:** 20% (24% for residential property)

## ISA and Savings Changes 2025-2026

### ISA Allowances

- **Overall ISA Limit:** £20,000
- **Stocks & Shares ISA:** £20,000
- **Cash ISA:** £20,000
- **Innovative Finance ISA:** £20,000
- **Lifetime ISA:** £4,000 (within overall limit)

### Personal Savings Allowance

- **Basic Rate Taxpayers:** £1,000 tax-free savings interest
- **Higher Rate Taxpayers:** £500 tax-free savings interest
- **Additional Rate Taxpayers:** £0 tax-free allowance

## Inheritance Tax 2025-2026

### Rates and Thresholds

- **Nil Rate Band:** £325,000 (frozen until 2028)
- **Residence Nil Rate Band:** £175,000 (when passing home to children)
- **Combined Allowance:** Up to £1 million for married couples
- **Tax Rate:** 40% above thresholds

## What These Changes Mean for You

### Low Earners (£20,000-£30,000)
- **Benefit:** Aligned NI and income tax thresholds simplify calculations
- **Watch:** Ensure you're not missing out on marriage allowance benefits
- **Action:** Review student loan repayments if applicable

### Middle Earners (£30,000-£50,000)
- **Challenge:** Continued threshold freezes mean effective tax increases
- **Opportunity:** Maximize pension contributions with higher annual allowance
- **Consider:** Scottish tax implications if relocating

### Higher Earners (£50,000+)
- **Significant:** New £60,000 pension annual allowance provides major planning opportunities
- **Alert:** High Income Child Benefit Charge still applies from £50,000
- **Plan:** Consider lifetime allowance removal for long-term pension strategy

### Very High Earners (£100,000+)
- **Complex:** Personal allowance tapering creates effective 60% tax rate
- **Opportunity:** Pension contributions can reduce adjusted income
- **Strategy:** Consider Scottish vs UK tax planning if location flexible
