## Optimization Checklist for PayeTax

### SEO Value

**Target keywords:**
- "[Amount] after tax UK"
- "UK tax calculator"
- "PAYE calculator"
- "Take home pay calculator UK"
- "[Amount] salary after tax"
- "Tax on [amount] salary"

**Page strategy:**
- Programmatic salary pages for common amounts
- Blog content for tax education
- FAQ targeting "how much tax on..." queries
- Seasonal content around tax year changes (April)

**Link building:**
- Original data/calculations attract links
- HMRC accuracy as differentiator
- Embeddable calculator widget for other sites
- Personal finance blogger outreach

### Lead Capture Strategy

**Current state:** Ungated (no email required)

**Options to consider:**

1. **Fully ungated (current):**
   - Maximum usage and SEO value
   - No direct lead capture
   - Brand awareness play

2. **Optional capture:**
   - Email to save/share results
   - Newsletter signup for tax updates
   - Tax year change alerts
   - Still high usage, some capture

3. **Partially gated:**
   - Basic result free
   - Email for detailed breakdown, PDF, or comparison
   - Higher capture, some usage friction

**Recommended for PayeTax:** Optional capture
- "Get your full breakdown by email"
- "Alert me when tax rates change"
- "Save my calculation"

### Shareability

> **⚠️ Privacy Note:** Salary and tax information is highly personal. Users will NOT share their actual payslip results publicly. Do not suggest social share buttons for calculation outputs, shareable result links, or "share your take-home pay" features. These feel invasive and won't be used.

**What DOES work:**
- Sharing the tool itself ("Check out this tax calculator")
- Sharing educational content (blog posts, guides)
- Sharing anonymized comparisons ("See how £50k compares")

**What to AVOID:**
- Social share buttons on results
- "Share your take-home pay" prompts
- Shareable URLs with salary pre-filled
- Any feature that exposes personal financial data

### Trust Signals

- "Matches HMRC calculations" badge
- Last updated date
- Tax year clearly displayed
- Link to official HMRC sources
- Transparent methodology
