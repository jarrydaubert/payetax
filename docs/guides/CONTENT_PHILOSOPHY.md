# Content Writing Philosophy
**Social-First Content That Converts**


---

## Overview

This document complements BLOG_GUIDE.md with a social-first content philosophy. While BLOG_GUIDE.md covers SEO, YMYL compliance, and publishing workflows, this guide focuses on **how to write content that stops the scroll and builds genuine audience trust**.

The core insight: a brilliant tax insight doesn't automatically become great content. The writing matters as much as the information.

---

## The Five Pillars of High-Performing Content

### 1. Genuinely Valuable (Not Recycled)

**The Problem:**
Most tax content online is the same recycled advice repeated endlessly:
- "Use your ISA allowance"
- "Check your tax code"
- "Contribute to your pension"

This doesn't make anyone stop scrolling.

**The Solution:**
Dig into experience and pull out fresh insights:
- Specific numbers that surprise ("You'll lose £23,000 in allowances on April 5th")
- Counter-intuitive findings ("Earning £110k leaves you with less than £99k")
- Real mistakes with real costs ("The £2,400 error I made for 3 years")

**The Test:**
> Would I bookmark this if someone else wrote it?

If the answer is no, keep working on it.

---

### 2. Immediately Actionable

**The Problem:**
> "Wow, the new pension rules are insane!"

This might get views, but it brings no followers and zero business.

**The Solution:**
People don't follow you because you noticed something. They follow because you taught them how to use it.

Instead of pointing at information, provide a system:

❌ **Bad:** "The £100k tax trap is brutal"

✅ **Good:** 
```
How to escape the £100k tax trap (step by step):

1. Calculate your adjusted net income
2. Work out your excess over £100k
3. Divide by 2 (this is your lost allowance)
4. Contribute this amount to pension before April

Example at £110k:
- Excess: £10,000
- Lost allowance: £5,000
- Pension contribution needed: £5,000
- Tax saved: £3,000

Use our calculator to see your exact numbers: [link]
```

**The Rule:**
Every piece of content should answer "What do I do with this information RIGHT NOW?"

---

### 3. Sparks Natural Engagement

**The Problem:**
Begging for engagement looks desperate:
- "Like if you agree!"
- "Retweet to help others!"
- "Comment your thoughts!"

**The Solution:**
Structure content so engagement happens naturally.

**For Bookmarks:**
Use phrases that trigger the save reflex:
- "Here's how to..."
- "The exact steps to..."
- "Do this before April 5th..."

People see something useful → bookmark for later → keep scrolling. Your job is making that first line so clear that bookmarking is instinctive.

**For Replies (Two Approaches):**

**Option A: Take a Strong Stance**
> "Salary sacrifice saves more tax than any other legal method. Here's the proof."

People will argue or back you up. Either way, engagement pushes the content further.

**Option B: Invite Perspective**
> "This is the pension strategy I'm using. Curious if anyone's tried the alternative..."

Feels collaborative. People love sharing their own experience when given an opening.

---

### 4. Ridiculously Easy to Read

**The Reality:**
People scroll at insane speeds. You have ONE SECOND before they decide to engage or bounce.

**Hook Rules:**
- Short and punchy
- One line that signals clear value
- "How to X" / "Why X doesn't work" / "The X mistake you're making"

**Structure Rules:**
- One sentence per line where possible
- Creates natural rhythm
- Easier to process visually

**List Formatting:**
Use `-` or `>` or `1.` to organize complex information:
```
> You're about to lose these allowances:

- £20,000 ISA (gone forever)
- £60,000 pension (partial carry-forward)
- £3,000 CGT (no rollover)
- £500 dividend (use it or lose it)
```

**White Space:**
The space between lines gives eyes a place to rest. Solid blocks of text feel exhausting before you even start reading.

**Language:**
- "Use" not "utilize"
- "Help" not "facilitate"
- "Get better" not "optimize performance"

**The Test:**
If your 14-year-old cousin couldn't understand it, it's too complex. Simplify until it feels almost too simple, then stop.

---

### 5. Recognizable Style

**The Problem:**
Same hooks, same structure, same voice everywhere. It all blends into generic content soup.

**The Solution:**
Develop patterns people recognize before they see your name.

**PayeTax Style Elements:**

1. **Lead with the £ amount at stake**
   - "You're about to lose £23,000..."
   - "This mistake costs £2,400/year..."
   - "Save £6,000 with this one change..."

2. **Use `>` for key insights**
   > This is the critical point most people miss

3. **End with calculator CTA**
   - "See your exact numbers: [link]"
   - "Use our calculator to check yours"

4. **Conversational acknowledgment of complexity**
   - "Here's where it gets messy..."
   - "Yes, you read that right..."
   - "Thanks, HMRC..."

5. **Specific examples, always**
   - Never just "higher earners" → "Earning £110k?"
   - Never just "tax savings" → "Save £3,000"

**Consistency Without Being Formulaic:**
Use these patterns frequently but mix up the order. Style should feel like YOU, not a template.

## Blog Post to Social Promotion Pipeline

### The Transformation

**Blog Post Title:**
"Year-End Tax Planning 2025-26: The Complete Checklist Before April"

**Tweet Version:**
```
> You're about to lose £23,000 in tax allowances on April 5th

Here's exactly how to use every single one before midnight:

- £20,000 ISA allowance (gone forever)
- £60,000 pension allowance (partial carry-forward)  
- £3,000 CGT allowance (no rollover)
- £500 dividend allowance (use it or lose it)

Full checklist with exact steps: [link]
```

### The Formula

1. **Hook:** Start with the cost of NOT acting (specific £ amount)
2. **List:** 3-5 most impactful items from the post
3. **CTA:** Link to full guide + calculator

### What Changes Between Blog and Social

| Element | Blog Post | Social Post |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Length | 2,000-3,000 words | 280 chars - thread |
| Depth | Full explanation | Key insight only |
| Structure | Headers, sections | One point per line |
| CTA | Multiple internal links | One clear link |
| Tone | Comprehensive guide | Urgent/actionable hook |

## Content Ideas That Apply This Philosophy

### Strong Hook Examples

❌ **Weak:** "Understanding UK Tax Codes"
✅ **Strong:** "Your tax code is probably wrong. Here's how to check in 30 seconds."

❌ **Weak:** "Marriage Allowance Explained"
✅ **Strong:** "Married? You're missing £252/year. Here's how to claim it today."

❌ **Weak:** "Pension Tax Relief Guide"
✅ **Strong:** "Higher rate taxpayer? Every £100 you contribute only costs you £60. Here's why."

❌ **Weak:** "Student Loan Repayment Changes"
✅ **Strong:** "Plan 5 graduates: you're now paying £0 until you earn £25k. Here's what changed."

### Fresh Angle Ideas

Instead of another "how tax works" post:

1. **Experience-based framing:**
   - "I calculated my tax wrong for 3 years. Here's the £2,400 mistake."
   - "I reviewed 500 tax returns. These 7 allowances get missed every year."

2. **Counter-intuitive insights:**
   - "Why earning £110k leaves you with less than earning £99k"
   - "The 60% tax rate that isn't in any HMRC document"

3. **Urgency-based:**
   - "31st January is 3 weeks away. Here's your 20-minute tax return checklist."
   - "100 days until your allowances expire. Here's the priority order."

4. **Comparison/controversy:**
   - "Scotland vs England: Who actually pays more tax? (It's not who you think)"
   - "Salary sacrifice vs pension contributions: The maths that settles it"

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify:

- [ ] **Value:** Would I bookmark this if someone else wrote it?
- [ ] **Actionable:** Does it answer "What do I do RIGHT NOW?"
- [ ] **Hook:** Does the first line make someone stop scrolling?
- [ ] **Readable:** One sentence per line? White space? Simple words?
- [ ] **Style:** Does it feel like PayeTax (£ amounts, `>` insights, calculator CTA)?
- [ ] **Fresh:** Am I saying something new, or saying something old in a new way?

---

## Measuring Success

**Leading Indicators:**
- Bookmark rate on tweets
- Reply quality (genuine questions = trust building)
- Profile visits after posting

**Lagging Indicators:**
- Follower growth rate
- Click-through to blog
- Calculator usage from social traffic

**The Ultimate Test:**
Are people DMing about the content? Not just likes—actual messages saying "this helped me" or "I didn't know this."
