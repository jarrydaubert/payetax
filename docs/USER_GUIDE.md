## 💰 Basic Tax Calculation

### Step 1: Enter Your Salary

1. **Annual Salary**: Enter your gross annual salary (before tax)
   - Example: £45,000
   - Accepts decimals: £45,500.50
   - Maximum: £999,999,999

2. **Tax Year**: Select the relevant tax year
   - **2025-26**: Current/upcoming tax year
   - **2024-25**: Current tax year (April 2024 - April 2025)

## 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿 Scottish Tax Rates

### When to Use Scottish Rates

Use Scottish tax rates if you're a **Scottish resident** (live in Scotland for more than half the tax year). This affects your **Income Tax only** - National Insurance rates remain the same.

### How to Enable

1. Check the **"Apply Scottish Tax Rates"** box
2. Recalculate to see the difference
3. Compare with English rates using the toggle

### Scottish vs English Differences

**2024-25 Example (£50,000 salary)**:
- **English Tax**: £7,500
- **Scottish Tax**: £7,742
- **Difference**: £242 more per year

Scottish rates typically have more tax bands and slightly higher rates for middle-income earners.

- **Threshold**: £31,395
- **Rate**: 9% above threshold
- **Example**: £35,000 salary = £324 annual repayment

- **Threshold**: £25,000

## 💼 Pension Contributions

### Types of Pension Contributions

#### Percentage-Based
- Enter as percentage of gross salary
- Common rates: 3%, 5%, 8%, 10%
- **Example**: 5% of £40,000 = £2,000 contribution

#### Fixed Amount
- Enter specific pound amount
- **Example**: £200 per month = £2,400 per year

### Tax Relief Benefits

**Automatic Tax Relief**: The calculator shows your tax savings:
- **Basic Rate (20%)**: £1,000 contribution = £200 tax relief
- **Higher Rate (40%)**: £1,000 contribution = £400 tax relief
- **Additional Rate (45%)**: £1,000 contribution = £450 tax relief

### Annual Allowance

- **Standard Limit**: £60,000 per tax year
- **Excess contributions**: No additional tax relief
- The calculator respects these limits automatically

## 🏛️ National Insurance Categories

### Category A (Standard Employee)
- **Most common category** for employees
- Standard rates: 12% (earnings £12,570-£50,270), 2% above

### Category B (Married Women's Reduced Rate)
- **Reduced rates** for married women with certificates
- Lower contributions but reduced benefit entitlement

### Category C (Over State Pension Age)
- **No National Insurance** if over state pension age
- Applies regardless of income level

### Category H (Apprentices Under 25)
- **Reduced rates** for registered apprentices
- Applies until 25th birthday

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### Tax Calculations

**Q: Are the tax rates up to date?**  
A: Yes! We update rates immediately when HMRC announces changes. Current data includes 2025-26 rates.

**Q: Can I calculate previous tax years?**  
A: Yes, we support 2023-24, 2024-25, and 2025-26 tax years with historical rates.

**Q: Do Scottish tax rates apply to me?**  
A: If you live in Scotland for more than half the tax year, you're a Scottish taxpayer and should use Scottish rates.

### Student Loans

**Q: Which student loan plan am I on?**  
A: Check your Student Loans Company statement or:
- Started university pre-2012: Plan 1
- Started 2012-2022: Plan 2  
- Started 2023+: Plan 5
- Scotland: Plan 4

**Q: Can I have multiple student loan plans?**  
A: Yes, you can have undergraduate + postgraduate loans simultaneously.

### Pensions

**Q: Should I use percentage or fixed amount?**  
A: Most workplace pensions use percentages. Use fixed amounts for personal pensions or SIPP contributions.

**Q: What's the maximum I can contribute?**  
A: £60,000 per year (2024-25) including tax relief, subject to earnings limitations.

### Results & Accuracy

**Q: How accurate are the calculations?**  
A: Our calculations use official HMRC rates and formulas. Results typically match HMRC calculators within £1-2 due to rounding.

**Q: Why don't results match my payslip exactly?**  
A: Payslips may include:
- Company car benefits
- Private medical insurance
- Different tax codes
- Overtime or bonus payments

**Q: Can I save my calculations?**  
A: Currently, you can export to Excel or bookmark the URL with your inputs for future reference.

1. **Gather information**: Latest P60, student loan details, pension info
