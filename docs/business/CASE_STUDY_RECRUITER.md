## The Problem: "Pension Gap" Zone

This director's salary of £5,845 falls in the **worst possible zone**:

| Threshold | Amount | Director's Position |
|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| Secondary Threshold (ST) | £5,000 | ✅ Above — paying Employer NI |
| Lower Earnings Limit (LEL) | £6,500 | ❌ Below — NO State Pension credits |

### The Maths

**Current state (£5,845 salary):**
- Employer NI: (£5,845 - £5,000) × 15% = **£126.75/year**
- State Pension credits: **NONE**
- Result: **Paying tax for zero benefit**

**If salary was £6,500:**
- Employer NI: (£6,500 - £5,000) × 15% = **£225/year**
- State Pension credits: **YES — qualifying year secured**
- Extra cost: £225 - £127 = **£98/year (£8.17/month)**

### The Trade-Off

| Salary | Employer NI | Pension Credits | Monthly Commitment |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| £5,000 | £0 | ❌ No | £417/month |
| £5,845 (actual) | £127 | ❌ No | £487/month |
| **£6,500** | £225 | ✅ Yes | £542/month |
| £12,570 | £1,135 | ✅ Yes | £1,048/month |

**The £5,000-£6,500 zone is the WORST place to be.**

---

## Why The Director Made This Choice

The director's logic (valid but suboptimal):

1. **Cash buffer priority:** £1,778 retained = 3.3 months of £533 salary runway
2. **Single client risk:** If client leaves, need buffer to survive
3. **Commission uncertainty:** Can't commit to higher fixed salary

**The flaw:** They didn't realise the £5,845 salary sits in the "paying tax for nothing" zone.

### What They Should Have Done

| Option | Salary | Buffer After | Pension | Extra Cost |
|--------|--------|--------------|---------|------------|
| Actual | £5,845 | £1,778 (3.3mo) | ❌ | — |
| **Recommended** | £6,500 | £1,123 (2.1mo) | ✅ | £98/year |

For **£8/month extra**, they could have:
- Secured a State Pension qualifying year
- Still maintained 2+ months runway
- Avoided "paying tax for nothing"

## Key Lessons

### 1. The Pension Gap Warning

**If salary is between £5,000 and £6,500:**
> "⚠️ Inefficient Zone: You're paying Employer NI but earning NO State Pension credits.
> Increase salary to £6,500 (+£55/month) to secure a qualifying year."

### 2. Cash Buffer ≠ Retained Earnings

- **Retained earnings (accounting):** £1,778
- **Cash in bank (reality):** £889
- **Creditors due:** £740

The director has **less runway than they think**.

### 3. April 2025 Threshold Change Impact

The Employer NI threshold drop (£9,100 → £5,000) means:
- More directors now pay Employer NI on low salaries
- The "Pension Gap" zone (£5k-£6.5k) is now a tax trap
- Previously "safe" salaries of ~£6k now incur NI but miss pension credits

### 4. Variable Income Needs Different Advice

Standard "optimal extraction" assumes:
- ✅ Known annual revenue
- ✅ Year-end extraction
- ❌ Variable/commission income
- ❌ Cash buffer requirements

For commission-based directors, the goal shifts from "max take-home" to "sustainable extraction with runway".
