# UK Director Tool Competitor Gap Analysis

> **Research completed:** 2026-01-27
> **Source docs:** `DIRECTOR_GUIDE_POSITIONING.md`, `DIRECTOR_CALCULATOR_BUILD.md`

---

## Executive Summary

**PayeTax's Director Guide has a significant competitive advantage.** No existing UK competitor combines education-first content with interactive calculators AND practical "set-aside" guidance. The market is full of:
- **Calculators without education** (most competitors)
- **Education without calculators** (blog articles)
- **Neither has "tax set-aside" features** (the gap)

## What PayeTax Is Building

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Education-first approach** | Explain concepts BEFORE calculating |
| **Entry choice routing** | "I'm new" vs "I know basics" paths |
| **Tax Bathtub visualization** | Visual metaphor for money flow |
| **Set-aside pots** | "What to ring-fence for tax" - the killer feature |
| **Key dates + calendar** | CT/SA deadlines with .ics downloads |
| **Sleep at Night status** | Emotional reassurance, not just numbers |

**Target:** First-time directors who need **clarity**, not optimization.

## The Competitive Moat

### What NO competitor has:

1. **"Set-aside pots"** - Zero competitors show "put £X in a tax savings account"
2. **Calendar .ics downloads** - No one offers "Add to Calendar" for CT/SA deadlines
3. **Education + Calculator integrated** - It's one or the other, never both
4. **"Sleep at Night" status** - Emotional reassurance is completely absent
5. **Survival mode** - No one addresses "I have no profit yet"
6. **Bank transfer labels** - "Label as SALARY" guidance doesn't exist

### What competitors do well:

1. **SEO presence** - Established sites rank well
2. **Trust** - Crunch, FreeAgent have brand recognition
3. **Comprehensiveness** - Contractor Calculator has many inputs
4. **Updated rates** - Most are current for 2025/26

## Strategic Recommendations

### 1. Build the "anti-surprise tax system"

The killer differentiator is **set-aside guidance**. No competitor does this. Users want:
- "What can I safely spend?"
- "What must I save for tax?"
- "When is it due?"

### 2. Optimize for educational keywords

Target "how to" and "explained" queries, not just "calculator" queries.

### 3. Create an accountant-friendly resource

Make it easy for accountants to point clients to one canonical explainer that reduces back-and-forth.

### 4. Consider comparison pages later

Once live, create:
- `/alternatives/gov-uk-calculator`
- `/vs/salary-calculator`
- `/best-uk-director-tools`

### 5. Highlight unique features in marketing

Lead with:
- "Finally understand how to pay yourself"
- "Know exactly what to save for tax"
- "Never be surprised by a tax bill"
