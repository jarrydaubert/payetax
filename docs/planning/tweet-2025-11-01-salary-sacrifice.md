## Tweet Content

```
Want to save £1,000s in tax while boosting your pension? Here's how salary sacrifice works 🧵

(Spoiler: £40k salary + £3k sacrifice = £840 saved + bigger pension. Legally. HMRC-approved.)

Let me break it down:

1️⃣ What It Is:

You agree to reduce your gross salary (before tax) in exchange for pension contributions.

Example:
• £40k salary → agree to £37k
• £3k goes straight to pension
• Tax/NI calculated on £37k, not £40k

Magic happens ✨

2️⃣ The Savings Breakdown:

Standard pension contribution from net pay:
• Pay £3k after tax
• Get £750 tax relief back
• Total in pension: £3,750

Salary sacrifice:
• £3k goes in before tax
• Save £600 income tax + £240 NI = £840
• Employer saves £414 NI (often added to your pension!)
• Total benefit: £4,254+

That's 52% return instantly 📈

3️⃣ Who Wins Biggest:

✅ Higher rate taxpayers (40%): Save £2,100+ on £5k sacrifice
✅ £100k-£125k earners: Escape 60% tax trap, save £8,714+
✅ Basic rate (20%): Still save 28% (£560 on £2k sacrifice)

Even basic-rate taxpayers benefit!

4️⃣ Real Example - £60k Salary:

Sacrifice £5k to pension:

💷 Save £2,000 income tax
💷 Save £100 National Insurance
💷 Employer adds £690 NI savings to pension

Total: £2,100 in your pocket + bigger pension pot

Every. Single. Year.

5️⃣ Watch Out For These:

⚠️ Reduces gross salary (matters for mortgages!)
⚠️ Can affect maternity/paternity pay calculations
⚠️ Drops student loan repayments (good or bad depending on your strategy)
⚠️ Can't easily reverse mid-year

Time it carefully 🎯

6️⃣ How to Start:

1. Ask HR: "Do we offer salary sacrifice pensions?"
2. Decide your amount (10-15% of salary is sweet spot)
3. Sign the agreement (reviewed annually)
4. Watch your first payslip to verify savings

Most employers already offer it!

7️⃣ Quick Win:

Even if you only sacrifice 5% of your salary, you're saving tax AND National Insurance while building a bigger pension.

£30k salary + £1,500 sacrifice = £420 saved + pension boost

That's £420 for a weekend getaway. Every. Single. Year. 🏖️
```
