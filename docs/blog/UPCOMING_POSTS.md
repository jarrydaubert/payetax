## Research Notes

### Fiscal Drag Post (Feb 2026)

**Verified data:**

| Year | Personal Allowance | Basic Rate Band | Higher Rate Threshold |
|------|-------------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| 2010/11 | £6,475 | £37,400 | £43,875 |
| 2025/26 | £12,570 | £37,700 | £50,270 |
| Change | +£6,095 (+94%) | +£300 (+0.8%) | +£6,395 (+14.6%) |

**Inflation 2010→2025:** ~64% (CPI)

**If threshold tracked inflation:** £43,875 × 1.64 = **~£72,000**

**Key points:**
- Basic rate band frozen at ~£37,700 is BY DESIGN (to fund personal allowance increase)
- Personal allowance nearly doubled to help lower earners
- But fiscal drag is real: 4.3 million more people now pay 40% tax
- The "stealth tax" effect is the frozen threshold, not the band size

**Common misconceptions to address:**
- £37,700 is NOT the higher rate threshold (it's just the basic rate band)
- The threshold is £50,270 (PA + basic rate band)
- Doubling PA was meant to help, but froze the higher rate threshold relative to inflation
