# Content Philosophy

Purpose: every PayeTax article should make tax less confusing without pretending to be personal advice.

This is not a growth-content machine. Blog content exists to support the calculator, explain UK tax clearly, and build trust through accuracy.

## Principles

- **Real value:** teach something concrete or remove a specific confusion.
- **Actionable:** the reader should know what to check, calculate, or read next.
- **Plain language:** define jargon before using it.
- **Trust-first:** cite or verify official sources for unstable claims, and avoid exaggeration.
- **Consistent voice:** calm, helpful, practical, and direct.
- **Visual consistency:** article images should feel like part of the same Ledger editorial set, not a mixed gallery of unrelated AI styles.

## Guardrails

- Do not hardcode tax rates or thresholds unless the value is the topic of the article and has just been source-checked.
- Avoid sensational framing around tax, debt, penalties, or deadlines.
- Avoid "save thousands", "free money", or certainty-heavy claims unless the assumptions are explicit.
- Be honest about calculator limitations.
- Use UK English as the default editorial style. Replace American spellings and terms unless a quoted source requires the original wording.
- Do not invent tax behaviour, provider support, production status, usage metrics, security controls, or official endorsement.

## Reader Promise

Every post should answer three questions:

1. What is the current, source-checked position?
2. What does it mean for a normal PAYE, director, landlord, self-employed, or student-loan reader?
3. What should the reader calculate or verify next?

If an article cannot answer those questions, refresh it, narrow it, merge it, or retire it.
